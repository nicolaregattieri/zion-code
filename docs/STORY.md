# Zion

### The summit where code meets clarity.

---

## The Mountain

There is a place above the noise. Above the tangled diffs, the forgotten stashes, the merge conflicts at 2 a.m. Above the slow Electron shells and the browser tabs pretending to be desktop apps.

That place is a summit. And from it, you can see everything.

Zion is named after the mountain -- not as metaphor alone, but as architecture. A Git client built to give you the high ground: a clear, unbroken view of your repository's history, your branches, your work in progress. The kind of clarity that only comes from altitude.

> "At the summit, the path you walked makes sense."

Every lane in the graph, every commit in the log, every file in the tree -- visible, navigable, immediate. No buried menus. No five-click workflows. You open Zion, and you see the shape of your project as it truly is.

---

## The Word

Before it was a mountain, Zion was a word. In ancient texts, it meant a place of refuge, a stronghold, a promised dwelling. A place you arrive at after the long walk through wilderness.

For developers, the wilderness is real. It is the fragmented toolchain: one app for commits, another for diffs, a browser tab for PRs, a terminal window hiding behind everything else. It is context-switching as a way of life.

Zion is the promise that it does not have to be this way.

> "A place of peace for those who build."

One window. Code editor, terminal, graph, operations, clipboard -- all present, all reachable with a keystroke. Not because we crammed everything into one binary, but because a unified workspace is the only honest answer to the way developers actually work.

---

## What Makes Zion Different

### Native to the bone

Zion is built in Swift and SwiftUI. Not wrapped in Chromium. Not rendered in a web view. Every pixel is native macOS, from the glassmorphism surfaces to the keyboard shortcuts to the way the app feels under your fingers. It launches in under a second. It respects your system appearance. It belongs on your dock the way Xcode and Finder do.

### AI as a quiet partner

Zion does not use AI to impress. It uses AI to remove friction.

- **Commit messages** generated from your staged diff -- one click, context-aware, in your project's voice.
- **Pre-commit code review** that catches bugs, security issues, and style drift before they reach the remote.
- **Merge conflict resolution** that reads both sides and proposes a semantically correct merge, not a coin flip.
- **Diff explanations** that tell you intent, risk, and narrative -- not just what changed, but why it matters.
- **Semantic search** over your entire history. Ask "when did we change the auth flow?" and get an answer.
- **Branch summaries, blame explanations, changelog generation, commit split advice** -- intelligence woven into every surface, never in the way.

You choose the provider. Claude, GPT, Gemini. Keys stored in Keychain. The AI works for you, not the other way around.

### The integrated terminal, done right

A real PTY. Login shell. xterm-256color. Split panes -- vertical and horizontal. Tabs. Independent zoom. Session persistence across repo switches. And a smart clipboard that watches your system pasteboard, categorizes what it finds (commands, paths, hashes, URLs, images), and lets you paste into any terminal pane with a single click.

This is not a toy terminal bolted onto a GUI. This is where you live.

### The graph that tells the truth

Color-coded lanes. Commit search by hash, author, or message. Branch focus with a double-click. Signature verification. Keyboard navigation. Paginated loading up to five thousand commits. Pending changes always visible at the top. The graph is not a decoration -- it is the central nervous system of the app.

### Operations without ceremony

Hunk staging. Line staging. Interactive rebase with drag-and-drop reordering. Cherry-pick, revert, reset. Worktree management with dedicated terminal panes. Submodule status, init, update, sync. Reflog viewer with one-click undo. Everything a senior engineer needs, nothing a junior engineer cannot learn.

## The Vision

Zion exists because we believe the Git client is the most important tool a developer touches every day -- and it deserves to be great.

Not adequate. Not "good enough for most workflows." Great.

> "The best way to work with Git on Mac is the one where every tool is within reach and nothing stands between intent and action."

We are building toward a workspace where:

- You never leave the app to accomplish a Git task.
- AI augments your judgment but never replaces it.
- The terminal is a first-class citizen, not an afterthought.
- The visual graph is not just pretty -- it is how you think about your project.
- Every interaction respects your time, your keystrokes, and your Mac.

Zion is not trying to be everything. It is trying to be the one place where version control feels like it was designed for humans.

## For the Developer Who...

**...thinks in branches.**
You maintain long-lived feature branches, rebase with intention, and care about a clean history. Zion's graph and interactive rebase were built for the way you work.

**...lives in the terminal.**
You are not afraid of the command line -- you prefer it. But you also want the visual context that a GUI provides. Zion gives you both in the same window, with split panes and a clipboard that understands what you copy.

**...reviews code seriously.**
You read diffs before you push. You want a second pair of eyes -- even if that pair is artificial. Zion's AI code review runs before your commits reach the remote, catching what linters miss.

**...works on a Mac and cares about it.**
You chose macOS for a reason. You notice when an app is native and when it is not. You want software that respects the platform: fast, quiet, beautiful, and out of your way.

**...is tired of context-switching.**
Editor here, terminal there, browser tab for PRs, another tab for CI. You want one window that holds your entire Git workflow. Zion is that window.
